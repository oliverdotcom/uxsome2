You must apply for a 1½-year Bachelor’s top-up degree digitally via our application form available on our website. You can apply from 1 February (study start in August) and 15 September (study start in January).

EU citizens pay no tuition fees for studying in Denmark. Non-EU citizens usually pay tuition fees according to the programme they are studying. (4-6,700 Euro per semester). SEE ALL THE TUITION FEES Expect to spend 700 – 800 Euro per month or more to cover housing, food and books.

There are no scholarships for any of the programmes at Business Academy Aarhus. For all students on full-time programmes SU - Danish State Education Grant Danish citizens can apply for financial support (SU) according to the rules for obtaining grants and loans from the Danish State Education Grant and Loan Scheme. Non-Danish citizens have to meet certain demands in order to be entitled to receive SU.

We strongly encourage our international students to learn Danish during their stay in Denmark. Your study programme is taught in English and you will be able to get by with just speaking English, however, when getting to know a new culture, it is very important to speak the language. Additionally, you will be looking for an internship at some point during your studies, and here, being able to speak Danish will provide you with a great advantage. The Academy cooperates with the provider UCplus. They offer classes which are free of charge at the Business Academy, flexibly organised to fit your schedule. As a new student, you will receive information about this option before the start of your programme.

You apply for all our Academy Profession Degree programmes and the 3½-year Bachelor Degree Programme that start in August through optagelse.dk/admission. You can apply from 1 February. For the programmes that start in January you can apply through our online application form on our website. Please note that only some of our programmes start in January. You can apply from 15 September.

If you have a Danish AP degree taught in English, you fulfill the English requirement. If you don't, you must have either English at Danish B level with minimum score 3.0 from a Nordic upper secondary examination or an officially approved English test. We accept these tests with the following minimum scores: IELTS: 6,5 TOEFL: 83 Cambridge C1 Advanced: 180 (formerly known as Cambridge English Advanced: Grade A, B or C) Cambridge C2 Proficiency: 200 Oxford Online Placement test: C1 (min. 85 in each part). We only accept this test when it is completed at Business Academy Aarhus. Sign up here Please note that the English test is valid for 2 years. For non-EU applicants, we only accept IELTS and TOEFL.
